About Me

[Photo Placeholder]

As a researcher at the intersection of cognitive science and educational technology, I focus on understanding how artificial intelligence can enhance learning outcomes while maintaining pedagogical rigour. My work combines experimental psychology, mixed-methods research, and data-driven design to develop tools that support both learners and educators.

I'm passionate about transparent research practices and believe in making science accessible. From pre-registering studies to sharing reproducible analysis code, I work to ensure that research findings can be trusted, replicated, and built upon by the broader scientific community.

Research Rigour & Open Science

Transparent and reproducible research practices are essential for scientific progress. I am committed to open science principles, from pre-registration to data sharing, ensuring that my work can be verified, replicated, and built upon by others.

Pre-Registration

All confirmatory studies are pre-registered before data collection, specifying hypotheses, methods, and analysis plans to prevent p-hacking and ensure transparent hypothesis testing.

OSF Pre-registrations →

Reproducible Analysis

Analysis code and computational environments are shared openly, allowing others to verify results and adapt methods for their own research questions.

GitHub Repositories →

Open Materials & Data

Study materials, instruments, and anonymised data are made publicly available when ethically appropriate, supporting replication efforts and meta-analyses.

OSF Materials →

Transparent Use of LLMs

When large language models are used in research or tool development, their role is clearly documented, including prompts, model versions, and limitations of AI-generated content.
