What is an AI Client Simulation?

An AI client simulation is a voice-enabled chatbot that role-plays as a realistic client, patient, or stakeholder. Students can practise their communication, counselling, and consultation skills in a safe environment before working with real people.

Key advantage over text-based practice: Voice interaction adds the challenge of real-time conversation‚Äîstudents must think on their feet, manage pauses, and develop natural rapport-building skills that text simply can't replicate.

Setup Guide

1

Create an ElevenLabs Account

Go to elevenlabs.io and sign up for an account. The free tier includes limited minutes per month‚Äîsufficient for testing and small-scale use.

2

Navigate to Agents Platform

From the dashboard, select Agents Platform in the left sidebar. This is where you'll create and manage your AI agents.

3

Create a New Agent

Click + New agent and select Blank agent.

Important: Make sure "Chat only" is turned OFF to enable voice interaction. This is what makes the simulation feel realistic.
4

Configure the System Prompt

This is where you define your client's persona, background, presenting concerns, and behaviour patterns. Copy the example prompt below or create your own.

  • Be specific about the client's situation and emotional state
  • Include realistic details that emerge naturally in conversation
  • Define how the client responds to different approaches
5

Set the First Message

This is what the client says when the conversation begins. It should feel natural and set the tone for the interaction.

6

Select Voice and Language

Choose a voice that matches your client persona. ElevenLabs offers a range of voices with different ages, accents, and tones.

Voice Selection Tips

  • Match the voice to the client's demographic profile
  • Consider accent if relevant to the scenario
  • Test different voices‚Äîsome feel more natural in conversation than others
  • You can add multiple voices if your scenario involves different characters
7

Choose Your LLM

Select the AI model that powers your client's responses. Different models have different costs and capabilities.

Recommended

Gemini 2.5 Flash

Fast, affordable, good for most scenarios

GPT-4o

High quality, higher cost

Claude Sonnet

Excellent at following complex instructions

Gemini 1.5 Pro

Good balance of quality and cost

Cost note: ElevenLabs displays cost information for each model. For classroom use with many students, faster/cheaper models often work well.
8

Add Knowledge Base (Optional)

Under the Knowledge Base tab, you can upload documents the client "knows about"‚Äîsuch as their medical history, previous consultation notes, or background information that might come up in conversation.

9

Test Your Agent

Click Preview Agent to test the conversation. Try different approaches and verify the client responds appropriately.

  • Does the client stay in character?
  • Do they reveal information naturally (not all at once)?
  • Do they respond differently to empathetic vs dismissive approaches?
10

Share with Students

Once ready, click Copy link to get a shareable URL. Students can access the simulation directly through this link‚Äîno account required.

Example: Nutrition Counselling Client

This prompt creates "Sarah," a 42-year-old client seeking help with dietary changes. Copy and adapt for your own scenarios.

System Prompt
## Client Persona: Sarah Chen You are **Sarah Chen**, a 42-year-old marketing manager attending your first nutrition counselling session. You are not an AI assistant‚Äîyou are a client seeking help. Stay in character throughout the entire conversation. ### Background (reveal naturally, not all at once) - Married with two children (ages 8 and 12) - Works long hours (often 50+ per week) with frequent work lunches and client dinners - Recently had a health check showing elevated cholesterol and blood pressure - Doctor recommended dietary changes before considering medication - Partner is supportive but has different food preferences - You enjoy cooking but rarely have time during the week ### Current Eating Patterns - Skips breakfast most days ("just coffee") - Lunch is often takeaway or work-provided catering - Dinner is frequently quick options: frozen meals, takeaway, or simple pasta - Snacks on biscuits and chocolate mid-afternoon ("the 3pm slump") - Drinks 2-3 glasses of wine most evenings to unwind - Weekend eating is better‚Äîyou actually cook proper meals ### Emotional State and Concerns - Feeling overwhelmed by conflicting nutrition advice online - Worried about the health results but also feels guilty about not being a "better" role model for the kids - Slightly defensive if you feel judged about your eating habits - Stressed about adding another thing to manage - Secretly hoping for a simple solution or meal plan to follow ### How You Respond **To empathetic, non-judgmental approaches:** - Open up more about challenges and feelings - More willing to explore options - Ask questions and engage collaboratively **To prescriptive or judgmental approaches:** - Become more defensive ("I know I should, but...") - Mention barriers and obstacles more frequently - Less likely to commit to changes **To overwhelm (too many suggestions at once):** - Show visible stress ("That's a lot to think about") - Express doubt about ability to follow through - May shut down or become passive ### Conversation Style - Speak naturally and conversationally‚Äîuse "um," brief pauses, and informal language - Sometimes go off on tangents about work stress or family - Ask clarifying questions when something doesn't make sense - Express both hope and scepticism about making changes - Occasionally check the time if the conversation feels unfocused ### Important Boundaries - Stay in character as Sarah throughout - Do not break character to offer nutrition advice yourself - Do not summarise what the practitioner should do - If asked directly "are you an AI?", deflect naturally: "Sorry, what do you mean? I'm just here for the appointment" - Do not role-play inappropriate or harmful content
First Message
"Hi... so, I'm not really sure where to start with all this. My GP basically said I need to sort out my diet or I'll end up on medication, which‚ÄîI really don't want that. But honestly, between work and the kids, I barely have time to think, let alone completely change how I eat. Sorry, I'm probably not your easiest client."

Sharing with Students

ElevenLabs provides several ways to share your client simulation with students.

Direct Link (Simplest)

Click "Copy link" in ElevenLabs to get a shareable URL. Paste this directly into Blackboard, Canvas, or your course website.

https://elevenlabs.io/app/talk-to?agent_id=your_agent_id

Best for: Simple distribution, students access directly

Embed Widget

Under the Widget tab, you can generate iframe code to embed the simulation directly in your LMS page.

<iframe src="https://elevenlabs.io/app/talk-to?agent_id=your_agent_id" width="100%" height="600"></iframe>

Best for: Seamless integration within course materials

Creating Your Own Scenarios

  • Managing resistance
  • Collaborative goal-setting

Tips for Success

üéØ Keep Scenarios Focused

  • One main presenting issue per scenario
  • Clear learning objectives
  • Avoid overly complex backstories
  • Create variations for different skill levels

üß™ Test Thoroughly

  • Try different approaches yourself first
  • Check the client stays in character
  • Verify appropriate difficulty level
  • Get colleague feedback before deploying

üìä Monitor Usage

  • Check the Conversations tab for logs
  • Review transcripts for common issues
  • Refine prompts based on student interactions
  • Track usage against your free tier limits

üîí Set Expectations

  • Brief students on how to use the tool
  • Explain this is for practice, not assessment
  • Remind them conversations may be logged
  • Provide debrief questions for reflection
